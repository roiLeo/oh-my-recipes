---
title: 'Herb-Roasted Prime Rib'
description: "A show-stopping centerpiece for your Christmas dinner. This herb-roasted prime rib is tender, juicy, and perfectly seasoned with garlic, rosemary, and thyme for an elegant holiday meal."
publishedAt: 2025-12-24
category: 'Main Course'
prepTime: 30
cookTime: 120
servings: 8
difficulty: 'medium'
image: 'https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1719329468408-cafbd16da8cb?w=900&auto=format&fit=crop&q=60&ixlib=rb-4.1.0&ixid=M3wxMjA3fDB8MHxzZWFyY2h8MTd8fHJvYXN0ZWQlMjByaWJ8ZW58MHx8MHx8fDI%3D'
imageAlt: 'Herb-roasted prime rib with vegetables'
ingredients:
  - '1 prime rib roast (4-5 pounds), at room temperature'
  - '4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened'
  - '6 cloves garlic, minced'
  - '2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped'
  - '2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped'
  - '1 tablespoon Dijon mustard'
  - '2 teaspoons sea salt'
  - '1 teaspoon black pepper'
  - '1 cup beef broth'
  - '1/2 cup red wine (optional)'
  - '2 tablespoons olive oil'
  - 'Fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs for garnish'
instructions:
  - title: 'Prepare'
    steps:
      - text: 'Remove prime rib from refrigerator 1-2 hours before cooking to bring to room temperature.'
      - text: 'Preheat oven to 450°F (230°C).'
      - text: 'Pat the roast dry with paper towels. This ensures a beautiful crust.'
  - title: 'Season'
    steps:
      - text: 'In a small bowl, mix softened butter, minced garlic, rosemary, thyme, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper until well combined.'
      - text: 'Rub the herb butter all over the prime rib, making sure to coat all sides generously.'
      - text: 'Let seasoned roast rest for 15 minutes.'
        timer: 900
  - title: 'Sear'
    steps:
      - text: 'Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat.'
      - text: 'Sear the prime rib on all sides until a golden crust forms, about 3-4 minutes per side.'
        timer: 240
      - text: 'Pour beef broth and red wine (if using) into the pan around the roast.'
  - title: 'Roast'
    steps:
      - text: 'Transfer skillet to preheated oven. Reduce heat to 325°F (165°C).'
      - text: 'Roast for 15 minutes per pound for medium-rare (internal temperature of 130-135°F).'
      - text: 'For medium, roast until internal temperature reaches 135-145°F.'
      - text: 'Use a meat thermometer inserted into the thickest part, not touching bone.'
      - text: 'Baste with pan juices every 30 minutes for extra flavor and tenderness.'
        timer: 1800
  - title: 'Rest and Serve'
    steps:
      - text: 'Remove from oven and transfer roast to a cutting board. Tent loosely with foil.'
      - text: 'Let rest for 15-20 minutes. This allows juices to redistribute for maximum tenderness.'
        timer: 1200
      - text: 'While resting, strain pan juices into a saucepan and simmer to create a rich au jus sauce.'
      - text: 'Carve roast into thick slices and arrange on a platter.'
      - text: 'Garnish with fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs. Serve with warm au jus.'
